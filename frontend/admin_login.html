Data Privacy & Compliance

Pursuant to the Republic Act No. 10173, otherwise known as the Data Privacy Act of 2012, the City Government of Koronadal is committed to protecting your privacy and safeguarding personal data processed by its administrative departments. Please read the terms for the respective offices below.

PESO

Public Employment Service Office

Privacy Statement

The Public Employment Service Office (PESO) collects and processes personal information (e.g., name, contact details, educational attainment, skills profile, work experience) for the sole purpose of facilitating employment facilitation services, job matching, referrals to employers, livelihood trainings, and tracking applicants under local/national employment projects (such as SPES). All data is shared only with verified, authorized employers and relevant government monitors.

Terms and Conditions

By logging into this portal as an authorized officer or accessing applicant records, you agree to:

  • Access applicant information strictly for job matching and livelihood validation purposes.
  • Maintain total confidentiality of applicant contact numbers, birthdates, and residential addresses.
  • Report any potential profile misrepresentations immediately to the PESO administrator.
  • Refrain from copying, downloading, or transferring data to unauthorized third-party platforms.
CSWDO

City Social Welfare and Development Office

Privacy Statement

The City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO) processes sensitive personal data (e.g., household composition, income logs, medical certificates, vulnerability classifications) to accurately determine client eligibility for emergency welfare grants, educational stipends, livelihood financial aids (such as AICS), and customized social services. Case documents are protected under high security standards.

Terms and Conditions

Authorized users managing CSWDO profiles acknowledge and agree to:

  • Handle all sensitive information with utmost discretion, respecting the privacy of individuals and families.
  • Submit true and validated assessment notes during case worker evaluation.
  • Execute validation background checks in compliance with social work ethics.
  • Avoid sharing cases, beneficiary documents, or vulnerability details with colleagues not assigned to the case.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I access my PESO or CSWDO account?
Authorized officers, administrators, and evaluators are assigned credentials by the City Government of Koronadal IT Administrator. Enter your registered username/email and password on the login screen to sign in.
What is the difference between PESO and CSWDO modules?
The PESO module handles employment, skills training, livelihood programs, SPES, and job matching. The CSWDO module handles case reviews, medical/financial assistance, and social welfare updates. Your account permissions limit you to your designated department.
What should I do if I forgot my password?
Please contact the IT System Administrator at the Koronadal City Hall to request a password reset, or submit an official ticket through the support channels.

Administrative Knowledgebase

Guide

PESO Program Management

Refer to these quick processing guidelines for PESO tasks:

  • SPES Processing: Require school certifications, birth certificate, and proof of family income. Progress can be updated from 'Pending' to 'For Selection' to 'Approved'.
  • Job Fair Referral: Match applicant skills using the portal's query engine before referring to active employers.
  • Livelihood Training: Log TESDA qualification profiles for tracing applicant graduation and subsequent job placement.

CSWDO Program Management

Refer to these social worker case management procedures:

  • AICS Eligibility: Scan medical prescriptions, social case study reports, or funeral contracts. Maximum aid is governed by the municipal CSWDO budget allocation logic.
  • Senior & PWD Verification: Cross-check Philsys or OSCA cards to ensure no duplication of monthly benefits.
  • Livelihood Assistance: Review business plans submitted by community groups prior to releasing starter kits or funds.
